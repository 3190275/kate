Artists will use their unique form of artwork to pass along their views and feelings. The importance and value of art and the artist to society has remained constant throughout history. ARTISTS



ANNIE
CABIGTING.

Filipino Artist Annie Cabigting explores the concept of appropriation to examine an artworks intrinsic character. Her well-known paintings depict people looking at paintings created by big name artists like Jackson Pollock, Vermeer, Mark Rothko, and Chuxk Cloase. Turning iconic artwoks into elements of our vicual and intellectual lanscape, Cabigting treats these paintings as part of the mise-en-scene of the museums, galleries, or auction houses she depicts.

BENEDICTO
CABRERA.

Benedicto Cabrera, better known as BenCab, is considered the preeminent Filipino painter of his generation. Primarily figurative, Cabrera’s work often depicts woman and occasionally men wrapped in swirling, bundled fabrics and capes. He was born on April 10, 1942 in Malabon, Philippines, and studied at the University of the Phillippines. In 1963, he won first prize for his oil painting of a talipapa, or market, in a student competition, and was hired soon after by the national paper as its Sunday illustrator.

HERMES
ALEGRE.

Multi-awarded artist Hermes Alegre was born in Daet, Camarines Norte on March 7, 1968. He graduated from the Philippine Women’s University, Majoring in Fine Arts, where he studied under notable artists Ibarra dela Rosa and Mars Galang. Art critics have described his works as “portrait-like renditions of women with an uncanny sense of movement.” Looking at his paintings, one gets the impression that the figures are actually alive. His paintings are mostly beautiful dusky women depicted in a fantasy of foliage executed with an abundance of colors and zestful energy.

ANDRES
BARRIOQUINTO.

Evolution is a word that can best describe the current state of Andres Barrioquinto's art practice. Whereas early in his career Barrioquinto intentionally shied away from Realism, he has now embraced it, in an attempt to stand apart from his contemporaries. His output, he also says, is now more personal. Barrioquinto was born in Manila but his family kept on switching residencies due to the nature of his father's job, which was writing and editing.
